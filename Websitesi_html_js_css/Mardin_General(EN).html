GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT OF MARDIN

      Mardin province is located in the Tigris Section of the Southeastern Anatolia Region. It is neighbor of borders with Syria. It is surrounded by Syria in the south, Şanlıurfa province in the west, Diyarbakır and Batman provinces in the north, Siirt province in the northeast and Şırnak province in the east.
      Mardin is located in the province of the same name between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers in the Upper Mesopotamian basin. This area used to be called by ancient Greeks “the country between two rivers”.
      It was considered as one of the major depository of grains. Because of its rich soil, it was the birthplace of many civilisations. Agriculture and animal husbandry began in this basin and the earliest legends originated here.
      The legends of Noah’s Flood, the Prophet Idris (known in the Bible as Enoch), and many others are attributed to this land.
      It started out as a starter and country plan from a 1960's perspective. The population of the four districts to neighboring provinces in 1990 was 14.4%. Due to security concerns, he started to move to Mardin city from both parts of the province and other provinces.
      The urban population ratio is 60%, the rural population ratio is 43%. Districts with the highest population are Kızıltepe, Merkez, Nusaybin and Midyat, respectively. It has a young population structure. Compared to 2000, the rate of the population under the age of 15 is 44.8%.
      It is one of the provinces with the most differentiated population in Turkey. Kurds, Christian Assyrians, Sunni Arabs, Turks, Yezidis and Armenians live in the province. Over time, the Syriac and Yazidi population decreased due to immigration. A small number of Assyrians live in the Mardin and Midyat regions of Beirut, the capital of Lebanon.






      Mardin has a hot-summer Mediterranean climate (Köppen: Csa, Trewartha: Cs) with very hot, dry summers and chilly, wet, and occasionally snowy winters. Mardin is very sunny, with over 3000 hours of sun per year. While temperatures in summer can easily reach 40 °C (104 °F), because of its continental nature, wintry weather is still somewhat common between the months of December and March, and it usually snows for a week or two. The highest recorded temperature is 42.5 °C (108.5 °F).M
